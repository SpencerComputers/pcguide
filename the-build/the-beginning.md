You will also need a phillips head screwdriver and an anti-static bracelet. The anti-static bracelet is to stop us from damaging the parts \(from static electricity in your body\), the screwdriver is to screw things into place, the magnetic parts tray to keep everything organized on the workspace, the cable ties are to keep everything organized inside the PC case itself, and the thermal paste to keep the CPU cool during operation.

{% hint style="info" %}
An alternative to an anti-static bracelet however is to take your power supply out of the box and flip the switch on the back to the off position, and plug it into the nearest power outlet, this means the power supply is grounded and whenever you move your feet or walk around, touch that to avoid any static electricity damaging your parts.
{% endhint %}

Normally when building a computer, you build it in a specific order:

* Motherboard
* CPU
* RAM
* M.2 \(If applicable\)
* Cooler
* Case
* SATA Storage \(If applicable\)
* GPU
* PCIE Storage \(If applicable\)
* Power Supply
